Spark!

Where Curiosity & Creativity Ignite Solutions to Real-World Problems

Do you want to create things that will change the world? Do you want to create change that will impact the world?

Channel your passion by teaming up with dynamic local entrepreneurs to become a change maker in our community! Spark! is a place where you can develop an entrepreneurial mindset as you work to generate creative solutions to real-world problems.

At Spark!, you will learn the skillsets necessary to move an idea from light bulb to launch. Live and learn stages of ideation, business development, perfected pitch proposals and business implementation. Be a part of a valuable team and use your critical thinking skills to grow your ideas to the next level. Spark! will be an engaging, off-site, creative space where you will have access to business mentors and other community resources to help support you in your development of a start-up company, social enterprise or nonprofit organization.

Who You Will Work With

One of the most exciting components of this course is the opportunity to work with some of the most creative companies and universities around. The St. Louis entrepreneurial ecosystem is energetic, creative and growing. It consists of high-energy startup companies, social enterprises and corporate intrapreneurs. Both small and large companies will be providing mentors that can offer rich professional development for you as an aspiring entrepreneur!

Where You Will Go

As a student in the Spark! program, you will have the opportunity to attend several high-leverage, entrepreneurial events in the St. Louis area. Opportunities such as One Million Cups, St. Louis Start-Up weekend and other opportunities will be made available to all young innovators involved in this program. Students will also have the opportunity to visit other local incubators and collaborative workspaces in the St. Louis area.

    How do I apply?
  1. Applicants must complete the online application form located at: https://tinyurl.com/parkwayspark
  2. Applicants will submit a 60 second video pitch. Students have three options for their pitch:
    1. I am pitching an innovative product idea
    2. I am pitching an innovative service idea
    3. I don’t have a specific idea, but I want to pitch myself and how I could support the launch of a new product or service idea!
  3. Videos will be evaluated based on the following criteria:
    1. Innovation - Pitch identifies a compelling need and clear, original solution.
    2. Feasibility - There is potential for this product or service in the market or a potential need for your knowledge and skills in launching a product or service.
    3. Impact/Need - There is potential for the proposed concept or a need for this person to be involved in Spark!.
    4. Quality of Presentation - Style, Poise, Skill, Clarity, Creativity, Personality, Confidence.
  4. Based on the strength of the video pitches, applicants may be interviewed by local entrepreneurs to determine who will be a part of this exciting program.
  5. Acceptance letters will be mailed May 16, 2014.
    How to submit your video:
  1. Record a video of your 60-second pitch.
  2. Upload the video to a video hosting site (e.g., YouTube, Vimeo, Flickr, etc.)
  3. Send your video link to jstanfill@parkwayschools.net no later than May 1, 2014.
  4. You will receive a confirmation email upon receipt of your submission.
